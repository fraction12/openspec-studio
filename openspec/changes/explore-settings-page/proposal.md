## Why

OpenSpec Studio will likely need a settings surface as the product grows, but the scope is not clear yet. We should capture the idea without prematurely committing to navigation, categories, or exact controls.

## What Changes

- Explore a dedicated settings page or panel for app-level preferences and operational controls.
- Identify which settings belong globally, per repository, or per workspace.
- Keep the current product surface unchanged until we decide what settings users actually need.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities
- `app-settings`: Future settings navigation, grouping, and preference management.

### Modified Capabilities

## Impact

- No implementation yet.
- Needs product exploration before specs/design/tasks are finalized.
- Likely related to persistent app data, repo preferences, validation behavior, appearance, and diagnostics.

## Open Questions

- Should settings be a full page, modal, side panel, or command-style surface?
- Which settings are global versus repo-specific?
- Should settings include diagnostics, validation preferences, persistence controls, or only user preferences?
- Where should settings live in the current left rail/workbench structure?
